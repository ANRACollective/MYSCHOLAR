Law Careers in Malaysia 2026 — Beyond Just Being a Lawyer

Kerjaya Undang-Undang Malaysia 2026 — Lebih daripada Sekadar Peguam

Law in Malaysia opens more career paths than many students realise. The most visible is Advocate and Solicitor — private practice legal work, admitted to the Malaysian Bar after completing an LLB and the CLP examination and nine months of pupillage. But legal qualifications are used across a much wider range of roles. Government Legal Officers (Pegawai Undang-Undang) work in the Attorney General's Chambers, government ministries, and statutory bodies — entry is through the Public Service Commission and offers stable, structured career progression. In-house legal counsel roles at corporations, banks, and GLCs are another major path for law graduates, offering corporate hours and competitive pay. Syariah law is a distinct track — graduates of Syariah law programmes (from UIAM/IIUM, UM, or state Syariah institutions) can practise as Syariah advocates in the Syariah courts. Academic law (lecturing and research) is also a path for those who pursue postgraduate qualifications. MyJourney currently maps the Lawyer (Advocate and Solicitor) path in detail — with the CLP, pupillage, and private practice career ladder all laid out for Malaysian students.

Undang-undang di Malaysia membuka lebih banyak laluan kerjaya daripada yang disedari kebanyakan pelajar. Peguam Bela dan Peguam Cara adalah yang paling ketara — kerja undang-undang guaman persendirian, diterima masuk ke Bar Malaysia selepas melengkapkan LLB dan peperiksaan CLP serta pupillage selama sembilan bulan.
