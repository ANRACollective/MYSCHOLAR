Urban Planning Internship in Malaysia — Government and Private Options in 2026

Internship Perancang Bandar Malaysia 2026 — Pilihan Sektor Awam dan Swasta

Urban planning internships in Malaysia offer exposure to either the government side (policy, zoning, development control) or the private consulting side (preparing planning applications, environmental impact assessments, and masterplan work). The main government avenue is JPBD (Jabatan Perancangan Bandar dan Desa) — both at federal level and state level through the various state JPBD offices. Local authorities like DBKL, MBPJ, MBSJ, and Majlis Perbandaran across states also take in planning students for industrial training. On the private side, planning and urban design consultancies such as Akiprima, Perunding Alam Bina, and urban planning divisions inside integrated property developers are the main employers. What you actually do during a planning internship depends heavily on placement: government placements tend to involve more document review, application processing, and meetings; private consultancy placements are more production-heavy — GIS mapping, report writing, presentation preparation. Students from UiTM, UM, and UTM planning programmes have the strongest placement networks through their respective departments. Allowances are modest in government placements (sometimes none beyond a small elaun), while private firms typically pay RM 700–1,000 per month.
