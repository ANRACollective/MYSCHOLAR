What to Do After Getting a Scholarship in Malaysia — Next Steps Guide

Selepas Dapat Biasiswa — Apa Yang Perlu Dibuat Seterusnya?

Getting a scholarship offer is exciting, but it also comes with a set of administrative and legal steps that many students are unprepared for. Most Malaysian scholarship bodies — JPA, MARA, Yayasan Khazanah, Petronas, TNB, and others — require a formal acceptance within a set window, often 7–14 days from the offer letter. Missing this deadline can result in the offer being rescinded. After acceptance, you will typically need to submit a medical examination report from an approved clinic, certified copies of your results and IC, and in some cases a statutory declaration from your parents. For scholarships with a bond clause, you will also sign a legal agreement committing you to serve a specified period after graduation — read this carefully and keep a copy. For overseas placements, the sponsor usually arranges university applications on your behalf, or you submit applications jointly. Some students who receive a scholarship for a programme they are unsure about ask about deferment — policies vary, but most Malaysian scholarship bodies allow a one-year deferment for medical reasons; academic deferment is less common and not guaranteed.
