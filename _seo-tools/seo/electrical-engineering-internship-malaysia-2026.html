Electrical Engineering Internship in Malaysia — Where to Apply in 2026

Internship Jurutera Elektrik Malaysia 2026 — Tempat Mohon

Electrical engineering is one of the broadest engineering disciplines in Malaysia in terms of internship opportunities — the skills transfer into power generation and transmission, semiconductor manufacturing, telecommunications, industrial automation, and consumer electronics. Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB) is the largest employer of electrical engineers in the country and runs structured industrial training placements across its generation, transmission, and distribution divisions. Semiconductor and electronics manufacturing companies in Penang and Selangor — Intel, Infineon, Western Digital, Keysight, Renesas — take electrical engineering interns in test engineering, process engineering, and R&D support roles. Telekomunikasi Malaysia (Telekom) and Celcom Axiata offer placements in network engineering teams. On the engineering consultancy side, firms like Meinhardt, Aurecon, and WSP take EE interns for building services and infrastructure projects. For automation and industrial systems, companies like Omron, Siemens Malaysia, and Yokogawa place students in field commissioning and technical sales support roles. EE intern allowances vary significantly: government-linked companies like TNB typically pay RM 700–900; MNCs in Penang often pay RM 1,000–1,500. Most require a minimum CGPA of 3.0 and are in their second or third year of study.
