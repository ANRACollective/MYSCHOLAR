Types of Scholarships in Malaysia — What Every Student Should Know

Jenis-Jenis Biasiswa di Malaysia — Apa Yang Setiap Pelajar Perlu Tahu

Malaysian students encounter a confusing landscape of scholarship types, often not knowing whether what they are applying for is a full award, a partial bursary, a loan-scholarship hybrid, or a corporate sponsorship with employment strings attached. Here is a clear breakdown. Government scholarships (JPA, MARA, state government) are generally the most generous — covering tuition, living allowances, and sometimes flights — but are the most competitive and often come with a bond. GLC scholarships (Petronas, Khazanah, TNB, Telekom) operate similarly but are tied to specific employers. Corporate scholarships from banks and MNCs vary enormously in value — some are full-tuition only, others add a monthly allowance, and some include graduate placement guarantees. Foundation scholarships (Yayasan Sime Darby, Yayasan YTL, Yayasan Canselor) are often need-blind or partially need-blind and cover a single university or a list of institutions. Then there is the loan-scholarship hybrid: PTPTN is technically a loan, but top-performing graduates have historically been eligible for conversion to a scholarship (though the rules on this change periodically). Overseas scholarships from foreign governments — Commonwealth, Eiffel, DAAD, IsDB — are additional sources that many Malaysian students overlook.
