How to Become a Pharmacist in Malaysia — Full Career Path

Cara Jadi Farmasis di Malaysia — Laluan Kerjaya Lengkap

Pharmacy in Malaysia requires a four-year Bachelor of Pharmacy degree (B.Pharm) from a recognised institution — UiTM, USM, UPM, UM, and IIUM are the main public universities, while private institutions include International Medical University (IMU) and several others. After the degree, graduates complete a one-year pre-registration internship (either in government or private sectors), followed by registration with the Malaysian Pharmacy Board. Government pharmacists are posted to Ministry of Health hospitals and community clinics. Career paths include hospital pharmacy (clinical pharmaceutical services), industrial pharmacy (working in pharmaceutical manufacturing companies like Pharmaniaga or CCM), regulatory affairs (MDA — Medical Device Authority), retail community pharmacy, and academic/research roles. Entry-level government pharmacists start at around RM 2,800–3,500 per month. Senior hospital pharmacists with clinical specialisation earn considerably more. The pharmaceutical industry in Malaysia is growing — companies like Pharmaniaga, CCM Pharmaceuticals, and international MNCs like Pfizer and Novartis actively recruit Malaysian pharmacy graduates.

Farmasi di Malaysia memerlukan ijazah Sarjana Muda Farmasi (B.Pharm) selama empat tahun dari institusi yang diiktiraf. Selepas ijazah, graduan menjalani internship pra-pendaftaran selama satu tahun sebelum mendaftar dengan Lembaga Farmasi Malaysia.
