Scholarship vs PTPTN — What Malaysian Students Need to Know in 2026

Biasiswa vs PTPTN — Mana Lebih Baik untuk Pelajar Malaysia?

PTPTN (Perbadanan Tabung Pendidikan Tinggi Nasional) is a loan — not a scholarship. This distinction matters because many students treat PTPTN as a neutral funding option without fully accounting for the repayment obligation that begins after graduation. A PTPTN loan covers tuition and some living costs, and repayment begins 12 months after completing your course, at a service charge of 1% per annum. The total loan for a 3-year local degree can reach RM30,000–RM60,000 depending on the institution. A scholarship, by contrast, is a grant — no repayment required, except in bonded schemes where you commit to working for the sponsor for a fixed period. The difference in long-term financial position between a scholarship recipient and a PTPTN borrower is substantial: the scholarship recipient enters their career debt-free, while the PTPTN borrower begins with a monthly repayment obligation that reduces take-home pay for years.
