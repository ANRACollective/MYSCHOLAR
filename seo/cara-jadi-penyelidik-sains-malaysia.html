How to Become a Research Scientist in Malaysia — PhD Pathway, Employers, and Pay

Cara Jadi Penyelidik Sains di Malaysia — Laluan PhD, Majikan, dan Gaji

A research science career in Malaysia spans a wider range of settings than most students realise. The public sector employs the largest number of researchers through MOSTI-linked agencies such as MIMOS (microelectronics and ICT), SIRIM (standards and industrial research), Forest Research Institute Malaysia (FRIM), Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute (MARDI), and the National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency (NPRA). Public university academics — particularly those at research universities like UM, UKM, USM, UTM, and UPM — form another major category, combining teaching with research grant output. Private sector research roles are growing in Malaysia, primarily in the pharmaceutical industry (clinical research organisations, local pharma manufacturers), semiconductor design, and food technology. The typical pathway starts with a Bachelor's degree in a relevant science field, followed by a Master's degree (often funded through graduate assistantship) and then a PhD for those aiming at independent research or academic careers. PhD stipends at Malaysian public universities under SLAI (Skim Latihan Akademik IPTA) run at around RM 2,000–2,500 per month. Entry-level Research Officers (Pegawai Penyelidik, grade C41) in government agencies earn RM 2,400–3,800. Senior researchers with PhDs and publication track records command RM 6,000–10,000+. International funding — from Newton-Ungku Omar Fund, ASEAN-EU collaborations, and World Bank grants — is increasingly part of the Malaysian research ecosystem.

Kerjaya penyelidik sains di Malaysia merangkumi agensi MOSTI (MIMOS, SIRIM, MARDI), universiti penyelidikan awam, dan sektor swasta (farmaseutikal, teknologi semikonduktor). Laluan lazim: Ijazah → Sarjana → PhD. Stipend PhD di bawah SLAI sekitar RM 2,000–2,500/bulan. Pegawai Penyelidik gred C41 bermula pada RM 2,400–3,800.
