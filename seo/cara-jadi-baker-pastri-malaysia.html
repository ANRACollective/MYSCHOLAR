How to Become a Baker or Pastry Chef in Malaysia — Training, Business, and Career

Cara Jadi Baker dan Chef Pastri di Malaysia

Baking has seen genuine growth in Malaysia, driven partly by the artisan cafe culture that has taken root in KL, Penang, and JB since the early 2020s, and partly by demand for celebration cakes, custom cookies, and sourdough products through social media channels. Bakers and pastry chefs work in hotels (high-volume production bakeries), standalone bakeries, cafes, and confectionery companies — and a significant number operate home-based businesses supplying event orders. Formal training is available through culinary and hospitality programmes at institutions like Berjaya University College, Taylor's University, KDU, and Institut Kemahiran Belia Negara (IKBN). TVET-pathway options exist through GiatMARA's baking programmes and culinary arts diplomas at community colleges. Hotel pastry chefs in Malaysia typically earn RM2,000–RM4,000 depending on the hotel category. Self-employed bakers who build a loyal following through Instagram or TikTok can match or exceed this, though inconsistent order volume is a real challenge in the early years.

Kerjaya baker dan pastri sedang berkembang di Malaysia dengan budaya kafe artisan yang semakin popular. Latihan tersedia di universiti kulinari dan program TVET GiatMARA.
