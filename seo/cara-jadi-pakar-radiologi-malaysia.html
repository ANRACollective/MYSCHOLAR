How to Become a Radiologist in Malaysia — The Path from MBBS to Specialist

Cara Jadi Pakar Radiologi di Malaysia — Laluan dari MBBS hingga Pakar

Radiology is one of Malaysia's more consistently sought-after medical specialties, and it is routinely ranked among the top three highest-paid medical careers in the country. A radiologist is a medical doctor who specialises in using imaging technology — X-rays, CT scans, MRI, ultrasound, and nuclear medicine — to diagnose disease and, in interventional radiology, to perform minimally invasive procedures guided by imaging. The career pathway in Malaysia begins the same way as all medical specialties: a five-year MBBS (or equivalent) degree from an accredited medical school, followed by a mandatory two-year housemanship (practical training) in a Ministry of Health (KKM) hospital, and then medical officer (MO) posting in the general health service. After accumulating the required clinical experience as a Medical Officer, candidates apply for the MMed (Master of Medicine) Radiology programme — offered at Universiti Malaya (UM), UKM, USM, UPM, and UIAM. The MMed Radiology is a four-year postgraduate clinical specialist training programme conducted partly in the university's hospital and partly in affiliated KKM hospitals. Passing all components awards the MMed Radiology degree and a specialist registration with the Malaysian Medical Council (MMC). Following specialist registration, radiologists in the government service enter as Specialist (grade UD52) earning around RM 8,000–12,000 monthly. Senior radiologists in established KKM hospitals, particularly those with interventional radiology or neuroradiology subspecialisation, earn RM 15,000–25,000. Private hospital radiology consultants at major private hospital groups (KPJ, Pantai, Prince Court, Sunway) command RM 25,000–50,000 or more depending on caseload and subspeciality. Important distinction: a radiologist is a medical specialist; a radiographer (Juruteknologi Perubatan Radiologi) is an allied health professional who operates the equipment. The paths and qualifications are entirely separate.

Radiologi adalah antara pengkhususan perubatan yang paling dicari di Malaysia. Laluan: MBBS (5 tahun) → Housemanship (2 tahun) → Pegawai Perubatan → MMed Radiologi (4 tahun, di UM/UKM/USM/UPM/UIAM) → Pakar Berdaftar (MMC). Gaji pakar kerajaan (gred UD52): RM 8,000–12,000. Pakar hospital swasta: RM 25,000–50,000+. Penting: pakar radiologi (doktor) berbeza sepenuhnya daripada radiografer (profesion kesihatan bersekutu).
