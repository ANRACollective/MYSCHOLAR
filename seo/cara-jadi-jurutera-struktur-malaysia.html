How to Become a Structural Engineer in Malaysia — Buildings, Bridges, and Beyond

Cara Jadi Jurutera Struktur di Malaysia

Structural engineering is a specialisation within civil engineering that focuses on the integrity and load-bearing capacity of structures — buildings, bridges, towers, retaining walls, and industrial facilities. In Malaysia, structural engineers work primarily in construction consultancies, quantity surveying firms, government agencies like JKR (Jabatan Kerja Raya), and infrastructure project management companies. The distinction from civil engineering is important: civil engineering is the broader field covering roads, drainage, and utilities, while structural engineering is specifically concerned with how structures resist forces and loads. Most structural engineers in Malaysia graduate with a Civil Engineering degree and then specialise through their professional development. Registration with the Board of Engineers Malaysia (BEM) is mandatory for professional practice, and Fellowship with the Institution of Engineers Malaysia (IEM) is the senior professional mark. Starting salaries typically range from RM2,800 to RM3,500 for fresh graduates.

Jurutera struktur mereka bentuk rangka bangunan dan infrastruktur supaya tahan beban dan selamat. Di Malaysia, mereka bekerja di firma konsultansi, JKR, dan syarikat pengurusan projek pembinaan.
