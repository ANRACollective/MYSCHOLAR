Scholarships for Psychology and Counseling Students in Malaysia 2026

Biasiswa untuk Pelajar Psikologi dan Kaunseling di Malaysia 2026

Psychology and counseling is a growing field in Malaysia, driven by increasing national awareness of mental health — reinforced by the government's Mental Health Policy and the Clinical Psychology and Counselors Act framework. As the profession formalises, funding opportunities for psychology students are expanding. The primary government pathway is through the Ministry of Health (KKM), which trains clinical psychologists for placement in government hospitals — medical officers studying clinical psychology via KPP (Kursus Psikologi Klinikal) post-housemanship are funded through service schemes rather than external scholarships. For undergraduate psychology students, the scholarship landscape draws from general merit and need-based sources rather than psychology-specific awards: JPA scholarships cover psychology degrees at public universities, MARA covers psychology at selected private institutions for Bumiputera students, and state scholarship boards (Yayasan Selangor, Yayasan Johor, and others) fund psychology alongside other social science degrees. At the postgraduate level, the SLAI (Skim Latihan Akademik IPTA) provides funding for psychology lecturers at public universities to pursue Master's and PhD degrees. Private university scholarships — from Taylor's, Sunway, Monash Malaysia, and HELP University (which has a strong psychology programme) — include psychology in their merit award coverage. The International Association of Applied Psychology and the Malaysian Mental Health Association occasionally publicise small research grants and fellowships that psychology students can apply for. MyScholar recommends psychology students check both field-specific and general scholarship databases, as the field benefits from a wide funnel of non-specific awards.
