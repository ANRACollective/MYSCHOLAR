How to Become a PTD Officer in Malaysia — The Civil Service Fast Track

Cara Jadi Pegawai Tadbir & Diplomatik (PTD) di Malaysia

The Pegawai Tadbir dan Diplomatik (PTD) scheme is Malaysia's premier administrative civil service track — equivalent to the Administrative and Diplomatic Service found in many Commonwealth countries. PTD officers form the policy backbone of the federal government: they are posted to ministries, state offices, Malaysian embassies abroad, and regulatory bodies. Entry is through a competitive selection process run by the Public Service Commission (SPA), which includes a written exam (PTD exam), group discussion, and interview panel. The scheme is open to Malaysian graduates of any discipline, though Social Science, Law, Economics, and Public Administration graduates tend to be well-represented. Starting grade is M41 (previously Gred M41), with a starting salary in the RM2,800–RM3,600 range depending on qualifications. Progression to Undersecretary (KSU) level is possible for high-performers, and overseas postings to Malaysian missions are a feature of the role — not a guarantee, but realistic for those who perform well in early postings.

PTD adalah skim perkhidmatan awam persekutuan yang paling berprestij di Malaysia. Pegawai PTD berkhidmat di kementerian, pejabat negeri, kedutaan Malaysia di luar negara, dan badan kawal selia. Kemasukan adalah melalui peperiksaan SPA dan temu duga panel.
