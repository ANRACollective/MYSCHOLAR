How to Become an Interior Designer in Malaysia — Spaces, Clients, and Career Path

Cara Jadi Pereka Dalaman di Malaysia — Ruang, Klien, dan Laluan Kerjaya

Interior design in Malaysia sits at the intersection of creative vision and technical construction knowledge — a working interior designer needs to understand spatial planning, material specifications, building codes, contractor coordination, and client communication, not just aesthetics. The profession serves a market that includes high-end residential (luxury condominiums, linked houses, bungalows), commercial retail (F&B outlets, boutiques, shopping mall tenants), corporate offices, hospitality (hotels, serviced apartments), and healthcare facilities. The professional body is BIID (Biro Ikhtisas Interior Designers) — formerly the Interior Design Professional Association of Malaysia (IDPAM) — which registers interior designers and advocates for professional recognition of the field. The degree route is typically a Bachelor of Interior Design or Bachelor of Interior Architecture, offered at institutions including Taylor's University (which has one of Malaysia's stronger design programmes), Universiti Teknologi MARA (UiTM, with interior architecture as a key offering), UCSI, INTI, and private design schools like Raffles College. A Diploma in Interior Design is also a viable entry, often from DBKL-approved private colleges, and many designers work their way up from a diploma through portfolio-building. The technical side includes AutoCAD and 3D rendering software (SketchUp, 3ds Max, Lumion, Revit) — proficiency in these is often tested at interviews. Starting salaries for interior design graduates in Malaysia range from RM 2,000–3,000 at small design firms; experienced senior designers at established KL studios handling significant commercial projects earn RM 6,000–10,000. Freelancing is common at the mid-career stage, with project fees ranging from RM 8 to RM 30 per square foot for residential work, depending on design complexity and material specifications. The market for renovation contractors and interior designers has expanded substantially in post-pandemic Malaysia as residential renovation activity increased.
