Audit Internship in Malaysia — Big 4 and Mid-Tier Firms in 2026

Internship Audit Malaysia 2026 — Big 4 dan Firma Sederhana

Audit internships at Malaysian accounting firms are among the most structured and educationally consistent internship experiences available to accounting and finance students. The Big 4 — KPMG, Deloitte, Ernst and Young, and PwC — all run formal internship intakes in Malaysia, typically for 3–6 months. Applications usually open between September and January for placements the following year. In an audit internship, you will typically be placed in a client-facing engagement team assisting with substantive testing, audit sampling, working paper preparation, and some client interaction under supervision. It is genuinely useful preparation for an accounting career — you see real financial statements, real client files, and real pressure. Mid-tier firms in Malaysia like Baker Tilly, BDO, Crowe, and Grant Thornton also run internship programmes that are often less competitive to enter and may give you more direct exposure earlier. For accounting students targeting ACCA or ICAEW, an audit firm placement is practically essential — it is the most direct route to satisfying the practical experience requirement for professional qualification. Big 4 intern allowances in Malaysia are typically RM 1,000–1,500 per month; mid-tier firms often pay RM 700–900. All four Big 4 firms have significant offices in KLCC and PJ — a few also place interns in Penang and JB.
