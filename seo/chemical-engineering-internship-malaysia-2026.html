Chemical Engineering Internship in Malaysia — Industry Guide for 2026

Internship Jurutera Kimia Malaysia 2026 — Panduan Industri

Chemical engineering interns in Malaysia have a particularly rich set of industry options — the country's industrial base in oil and gas, palm oil processing, pharmaceuticals, and petrochemicals means genuine process engineering exposure is available. Petronas is the most prominent employer, with structured internship programmes at PETRONAS Chemicals Group facilities in Kerteh (Terengganu) and KLCC corporate office. The RAPID petrochemical complex in Pengerang, Johor operated by Petronas also takes interns. Palm oil processing is uniquely Malaysian — Sime Darby Oils, IOI Corporation, and Wilmar International have R&D and process engineering teams that occasionally take chemical engineering students. Pharmaceutical manufacturing is another sector — AstraZeneca, Hovid, Kotra Pharma, and Duopharma Biotech all have manufacturing plants in Malaysia and take interns in process and quality engineering. Specialty chemicals companies like BASF Malaysia and Clariant are smaller but offer more focused process chemistry exposure. For oil and gas beyond Petronas, companies like Shell Sarawak, ExxonMobil Malaysia, and the various upstream contractors place chemical engineering interns in offshore and onshore support roles. Allowances vary: Petronas typically pays RM 1,000–1,500; manufacturing company placements are often lower at RM 600–900.
