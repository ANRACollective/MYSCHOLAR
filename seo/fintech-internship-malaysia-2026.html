Internship Fintech Malaysia 2026

Malaysia's fintech sector has grown substantially since Bank Negara Malaysia's regulatory sandbox introduced digital banking licences. In 2026, licensed digital banks including GXBank (Grab-backed), AEON Bank, and Boost Bank are operating and hiring. Established fintech companies like Touch 'n Go eWallet, RinggitPlus, and PolicyStreet regularly take interns across engineering, product, data analytics, compliance, and marketing functions. MDEC's Malaysia Digital Economy agenda has also created funded placements through Digital Apprenticeship programmes for CS and data science students specifically. The fintech internship landscape in Malaysia sits between startups (flexible, fast-paced, broader scope) and established financial institutions (more structured, compliance-heavy, narrower role). For Computer Science and Finance students, fintech internships are one of the best ways to build a portfolio that appeals to both tech and banking employers.
