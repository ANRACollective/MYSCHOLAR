Agriculture and Agri-Science Careers in Malaysia — Palm Oil, Food Tech, and Precision Farming

Kerjaya Pertanian dan Agrosains di Malaysia — Sawit, Teknologi Makanan, dan Pertanian Presisi

Agriculture is more economically significant in Malaysia than its graduate employment narrative suggests. The country is the world's second largest producer of palm oil — a commodity that underpins a major industrial supply chain including oleo-chemicals, biofuel, and specialty fats manufacturing. The Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) employs researchers, agronomists, chemists, and engineers. Sime Darby Plantation, IOI Group, Felda Global Ventures (now FGV Holdings), and IJM Plantations are among the major private sector plantation employers. Beyond palm oil, Malaysia has significant aquaculture (prawn, tilapia, seabass), tropical fruits (durian export has become a major industry, particularly to China), rubber, and paddy sectors. MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) is the national body for agricultural research and technology transfer, employing researchers with degrees in Agronomy, Crop Science, Agricultural Engineering, Biotechnology, and Food Technology. The food technology subset is worth highlighting separately — Malaysia's processed food and halal food manufacturing industry is substantial, with Nestlé Malaysia, Yee Lee Corporation, Mamee-Double Decker, and hundreds of SMEs employing food scientists and technologists. UPM (Universiti Putra Malaysia) is the primary agricultural university, though UKM, UTM, and UMS also offer strong agri-science programmes. Starting salaries for agronomists and agricultural officers (Pegawai Pertanian, gred G41) in the government sector are RM 2,200–3,200; private sector plantation management roles can offer RM 3,500–5,000 for fresh graduates.

UPM adalah universiti pertanian utama. Gaji permulaan Pegawai Pertanian (gred G41): RM 2,200–3,200; sektor swasta: RM 3,500–5,000.
