Internship First Day in Malaysia — Tips That Actually Help

Hari Pertama Internship Malaysia — Tips Yang Sebenarnya Berguna

The first week of your internship sets the tone for the entire placement. Malaysian workplace culture has specific nuances that differ from what students expect based on lectures or TikTok advice. Here is what actually matters. Arrive early on day one — not 5 minutes early, 15 to 20 minutes early. In Malaysian offices, punctuality on the first day is read as a signal of seriousness, and being late is remembered longer than any good work you do later. Dress formally unless the company has specifically told you it is a casual environment — err on the side of overdressing on day one and adjust after you see what colleagues wear. Do not use your phone during meetings or briefings, even to take notes — ask for permission first or bring a notebook. Introduce yourself with your name, your university, and your programme. Do not wait for people to come to you — take initiative to greet colleagues in the pantry or common areas. Ask your supervisor on day one: what does a successful internship look like to them, what are the key deliverables, and who should you go to with questions. Write everything down in a notebook on day one — you cannot remember everything and it signals professionalism. MyIntern connects you to companies that take internship seriously.
