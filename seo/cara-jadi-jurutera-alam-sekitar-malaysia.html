How to Become an Environmental Engineer in Malaysia — EIA, ESG, and Clean Infrastructure

Cara Jadi Jurutera Alam Sekitar di Malaysia

Environmental engineering is gaining traction in Malaysia as ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) requirements become mandatory for listed companies and as government infrastructure projects increasingly require Environmental Impact Assessments (EIA). Environmental engineers work on water and wastewater treatment plants, industrial effluent management, solid waste systems, air pollution control, and contaminated land remediation. The Department of Environment (DOE) is the main regulatory body, and EIA reports — which must be submitted before major development projects can proceed — are a core output of the profession. Employers include environmental consultancies, water utilities like Pengurusan Air Selangor (PAAB) and Air Selangor, Indah Water Konsortium, and large construction companies with dedicated sustainability teams. Degree programmes in Environmental Engineering are offered at UTM, UPM, USM, and UNIMAP. Starting salaries are in the RM2,800–RM3,200 range, with senior EIA specialists earning considerably more.

Jurutera alam sekitar menangani rawatan air, pengurusan sisa, kawalan pencemaran udara, dan laporan EIA. Permintaan semakin meningkat seiring keperluan ESG dan projek infrastruktur kerajaan.
