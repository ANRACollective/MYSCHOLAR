How to Become a Statistician in Malaysia — Where Numbers Shape Policy

Cara Jadi Ahli Statistik di Malaysia — Di Mana Nombor Membentuk Dasar

Statistics is one of those professions that is perpetually undersupplied relative to demand in Malaysia, partly because it is underrepresented as a standalone degree and partly because many statistics graduates migrate into adjacent roles labelled 'data analyst' or 'data scientist'. A professional statistician differs from a data analyst in the depth of theoretical grounding — probabilistic modelling, survey design, experimental design, Bayesian inference — that underpins their work. The Department of Statistics Malaysia (DoSM or DOSM) is the most prominent public employer, responsible for conducting the national census, Labour Force Survey, Household Income and Expenditure Survey, and economic indicators like CPI and GDP. Bank Negara Malaysia and the Securities Commission also employ statisticians for macroprudential and market surveillance analysis. In the private sector, insurance companies (actuary-adjacent roles), pharmaceutical CROs (clinical trial statistics), and market research firms recruit statisticians. The degree route is a Bachelor of Statistics or Bachelor of Mathematics (Statistics) — offered at UM, USM, UKM, UTM, and UTeM. Starting salaries are RM 2,500–3,500 in government (Pegawai Perangkaan, S41) and RM 3,500–5,000 in the private sector. Statisticians who combine R or Python proficiency with theoretical statistics are among the most employable quantitative graduates in Malaysia today.

Ahli statistik mempunyai bekalan yang sentiasa kurang berbanding permintaan di Malaysia. Majikan utama dalam sektor awam ialah DoSM, Bank Negara Malaysia, dan Suruhanjaya Sekuriti. Ijazah Statistik ditawarkan di UM, USM, UKM, UTM, dan UTeM. Gaji permulaan RM 2,500–5,000 bergantung kepada sektor.
