Media and Communications Careers in Malaysia — Journalism, PR, Broadcasting, and Digital

Kerjaya Media dan Komunikasi di Malaysia — Kewartawanan, PR, Penyiaran, dan Digital

Media and communications is one of Malaysia's more versatile graduate fields — the skills developed (writing, storytelling, media production, public speaking, research, and strategic communication) are in demand across sectors that extend well beyond the traditional newsroom. The traditional media sector employs journalists and editors at newspapers (The Star, New Straits Times, Utusan Malaysia, Sin Chew Daily, The Edge), television broadcasters (RTM, Astro, TV3, NTV7, TV9), radio stations (BFM, Hitz FM, Era FM), and online news portals (Free Malaysia Today, Malaysiakini, The Malaysian Reserve). Journalists in Malaysia typically start at RM 2,200–3,200; senior reporters and editors at established outlets reach RM 5,000–8,000. The public relations and corporate communications track is often better-compensated than journalism — PR agencies (Edelman, Weber Shandwick, and dozens of Malaysia-based independent agencies) and corporate communications departments at PLCs, GLCs, and multinationals hire communications graduates for media relations, crisis communications, and brand management. PR executives start at RM 2,500–4,000; PR managers at RM 5,000–9,000. The fastest-growing segment is digital content and social media management — brands, agencies, and media companies need content strategists, social media managers, SEO writers, and video producers. This work pays RM 2,500–5,000 at the executive level, with experienced digital marketers reaching RM 6,000–10,000. Advertising and the creative agency sector (Leo Burnett, TBWA, JWT, Grey, and local agencies) absorbs communications, mass comm, and creative writing graduates into copywriting, account management, and campaign planning. Broadcasting — specifically video production, audio engineering, and live production — is a technical niche that benefits from specialised practical training, available at Multimedia University (MMU) and UNIKL MIIT. The degree of choice for most of these paths is Bachelor of Mass Communication or Bachelor of Communications from local universities — UM, UPM, UiTM, UCSI, and Taylor's offer well-regarded programmes.

Media dan komunikasi di Malaysia merangkumi kewartawanan (The Star, Astro, RTM — RM 2,200–8,000), perhubungan awam (Edelman, Weber Shandwick, jabatan komunikasi korporat — RM 2,500–9,000), kandungan digital dan media sosial (RM 2,500–10,000), pengiklanan (agensi kreatif), dan penyiaran. Ijazah utama: Komunikasi Massa dari UM, UPM, UiTM, UCSI, atau Taylor's.
